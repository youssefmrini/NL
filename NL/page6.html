BP Mission in London

by Mehdi Tantaoui

I went to London to join the Connected Innovation team that works mainly with BP Shipping. Their job is to promote innovative ideas, find sponsors for them within BP organisation and run a proof of concepts to test the feasibility and its value.

The team is made of Business Analysts, Designers, Strategy Consultants and AI specialists. As you might have guessed, I am part of the AI specialists and my role is to lead data science PoCs and provide guidance and

expertise for all AI ideas that comes across our team.

Now let’s talk a little bit more about what I do, where I am based and who I’m working with. 

But before going any further in reading, it is highly recommended to put this song on: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v2AC41dglnM
PreviousNext

Most often, the ideas we receive are sponsored by senior stakeholders within BP, that implies high expectations and believe me, that team is ready for the challenge.

Connected Innovation team is young (22 to 34 yd). They impressed me personally by the level of maturity in consulting and how big drinkers they are at the pub!

The location where we are working is very central, we have a nice view on the tideway and the London eye. The place is called WeWork, a trendy shared workspace. They are in 500 locations in the world. Our building has 16 floors with a DJ playing music at 8AM in the ground floor and free drinks in the 9th floor starting from 3PM (that’s crazy right?). Our day starts with a daily stand up, followed by a coffee break then everybody starts working on their tasks. Lunch time takes us to the food market! The afternoon is usually pretty busy running workshops and planning how every idea should be scoped. Afterwork => understudy: the pub for IBMers. The time to make new friends and create affinities with the team. What’s for dinner? Indian curry is pretty typical, I almost fainted when I dared to order the volcano hot curry ;)

The first PoC I was involved in was called Kadence. It’s a project that deals with medical reports. BP wanted to understand what are the health problems that the seafarers face when they are sailing. The data we used was free text word documents filled by the medical officers when an incident happens onboard the ship. The challenge was to extract diagnosis from the medical reports and aggregate them to have a global overview on the top occurring medical conditions. The team have received some fabulous feedback on what we produced. Now I am doing consulting in Data science in a wider range within BP Shipping and Trading.

At the time I am writing this, the adventure is still in its very beginning! We’ve earned the client’s trust and he’s eager to continue the journey with us to help them process and solve all upcoming challenges.

Cheers! 

Mehdi Tantaoui
